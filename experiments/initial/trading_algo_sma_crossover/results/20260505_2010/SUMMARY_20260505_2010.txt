  Why ATR-SMA-C over ATR-SMA?
    ATR-SMA-C adds a cash fallback (2% p.a.) when all alt assets are negative.
    In practice the difference is small (+0.1-0.5 pp CAGR) but it adds a safety
    layer in scenarios where even alt assets are falling simultaneously.

  Why sma=30, atr=10, mult=0.30?
    - Consistent #1 or top-3 across all three validation methods (grid, WF, K-Fold).
    - 30-day SMA: fast enough to exit bear markets, slow enough to avoid whipsaws.
    - atr=10: reactive but not noisy (shorter than typical 14-day default).
    - mult=0.30: tight ATR band — exit trigger is close to SMA (low lag).
    - Total trades on full TEST period: 32 (avg ~10/year) — manageable frequency.

  When would you prefer Buy&Hold instead?
    - If you believe markets will have multi-year uninterrupted bull runs.
    - If you cannot monitor/rebalance monthly (strategy needs monthly execution).
    - If transaction costs are very high (32 trades over 3 years is reasonable on IBKR).

  Bottom line:
    ATR-SMA-C(sma=30, atr=10, mult=0.30) is validated across train/test split,
    5 walk-forward windows covering COVID, bull, bear, and recovery, and 10 years
    of leave-one-out k-folds. It consistently outperforms Buy&Hold on a
    risk-adjusted basis. Use it.
